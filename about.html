About Rusangu

Rusangu University, formerly known as Zambia Adventist University, is a private coeducational Christian university based in Rusangu Mission near Monze in Zambia. It is owned and operated by the Seventh-day Adventist Church. This is considered one of the best universities in Zambia and in Africa region. This is known for its academic standards and research contributions. It is a part of the Seventh-day Adventist education system, the world's second largest Christian school system

Rusangu University is a private Christian institution in Zambia. Established in 2003, it offers a range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs in various fields, including business, education, theology, and health sciences. Affiliated with the Seventh-day Adventist Church, it aims to provide quality education within a Christian context, fostering holistic development and ethical values among its students.

The Seventh-day Adventist (SDA) Church is a Protestant Christian denomination founded in the 19th century. With a focus on biblical teachings, it emphasizes the second coming of Jesus Christ and observing the Sabbath on Saturday. The SDA Church promotes healthful living and operates schools, hospitals, and humanitarian projects worldwide.
